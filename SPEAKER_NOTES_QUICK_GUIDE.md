### Section 3.4: Resource Estimation System (1.5 minutes)

**The Ambulance Formula (Show, Don't Explain):**
```
Ambulances Needed = CEIL(Deaths × 0.3 + Injuries × 0.5 + Rescue_Time × 0.8)
```

**Why This Formula Works:**
- Deaths get lower weight (already critical care concept)
- Injuries get highest weight (primary ambulance demand)
- Time gets weighted (extended operations need more resources)

**Real-World Example:**
"Accident: 5 deaths, 30 injured, 3-hour rescue
Calculation: 5(0.3) + 30(0.5) + 3(0.8) = 18.9 → 19 ambulances

Current system: "We need ambulances, send what we can"
Our system: "We need exactly 19. Deploy from these 3 towns. ETA: 15 minutes"

Impact: The difference between saving someone's life and losing them is often just minutes."

**Damage Estimation:**
"Same model estimates infrastructure damage: ₹5 lakhs to ₹200+ lakhs depending on severity"

### "Your data is historical—does it apply to modern trains?"

✅ **Response:**
"Great question. Yes, modern trains are safer, but accident patterns remain consistent: time, weather, track condition, human factors. What changes is scale. Our model adapts as new data inputs arrive—it becomes more accurate over time, not less."

---

### "What about black swan events we can't predict?"

✅ **Response:**
"Our model isn't about predicting earthquakes or catastrophic weather. It's about the 63% of preventable accidents through maintenance and protocol. For the external 37%, our system still provides resource optimization—faster response saves lives even in unpredictable events."

---

### "This requires massive infrastructure changes—is it realistic?"

✅ **Response:**
"No infrastructure changes needed. Our system works with existing equipment. It runs in the cloud, provides recommendations to decision-makers, and integrates via standard data feeds. Implementation time: 3-6 months for pilot, 12-18 months for full rollout."

---

### "What's the failure mode if the system makes a wrong prediction?"

✅ **Response:**
"Our model recommends—humans decide. If we predict low risk but an accident occurs, we have redundant safety systems. If we predict high risk and nothing happens, we've done a cost-benefit: extra caution costs less than one accident. False positives are acceptable; false negatives could be tragic."

---

### "How do we measure success?"

✅ **Response:**
"Clear KPIs: (1) Accident reduction %, (2) Response time improvement, (3) Cost savings, (4) Lives saved, (5) Model accuracy trending. All measured monthly, reviewed quarterly. Pilot phase validates or invalidates assumptions."
